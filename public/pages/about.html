About SpectraFlux

SpectraFlux (formerly RandomWeb) is a free web proxy built on Corrosion by Titanium Network that allows you to bypass censorship and access blocked content. With its clean, user-friendly interface, SpectraFlux offers a fast, secure, and private browsing experience.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does SpectraFlux work?
SpectraFlux acts as an intermediary between your device and the internet. When you request a website through Corrosion, the proxy forwards your request to the target site, retrieves its content, and sends it back to you. This allows you to bypass network restrictions and access blocked content.
Is it encrypted?
Your connection to SpectraFlux is encrypted. When you access HTTPS websites, the data between the proxy and the target site is also encrypted, ensuring secure transmission.
Does it hide my IP address from the target site?
Yes, when you use SpectraFlux the target site sees our proxy server’s IP address instead of yours, providing you with a level of anonymity.
Can I access Netflix, YouTube, etc.?
While platforms like YouTube generally work with SpectraFlux, streaming services such as Netflix actively block proxy servers to enforce regional restrictions. You may encounter issues accessing Netflix.
Will it work on my phone, school Wi-Fi, or in my country?
SpectraFlux works on desktops and mobile devices via modern web browsers. However, network restrictions (such as on some school Wi-Fi networks) may sometimes block proxy traffic. In Canada, where there are minimal restrictions, it should work reliably.
